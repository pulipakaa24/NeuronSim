Why Did I Choose This Project?

With my mom being a neurologist and my dad an engineer, I've always ended up having a sort of identity crisis between biology and math/physics. Up until a few years ago even, I was teetering between becoming a doctor and becoming a mechanical or electrical/electronics engineer.

This continued until I realized that I could combine my interests in so many fields, such as BioECE. While there are many fields within BioECE that pique my interest, BCIs (Brain-Computer Interfaces) came to mind almost instantly as a potential application of diff-eq in signal processing. As I continued researching, however, I found that the core behavior of neurons was much more interesting than 'spike sorting,' as the classification of neuron signals is known. The modeling of membrane potential used diff-eq and was much more interesting in my opinion.

An Outside Perspective

Although my two options for BCI researcher interviews didn't quite get back to me, I had a good talk with my mother about how she reads EEGs in her work as a neurologist. It was interesting to see how much math trickles down to every STEM subject, albeit in watered-down forms. Essentially, there are a few different categories of brain activity waves, including alpha, beta, gamma, delta, and theta waves, all occupying different frequency ranges. My mother doesn't sit there and do calculations of period, but rather just recognizes their relative frequencies because she's simply seen so many of them. In this manner, she is able to care for patients without an exact knowledge of the math behind the waves.

This brings up an important point, that neuroscience is by no means an exact science. Just like many other biological systems, much of the behavior of a neuron is relatively random, and we can only approximate their true activity, or make educated guesses about how neurons would work in an ideal situation.

The Models

The Morris Lecar Model, which the simulation is built off of, was preceded by the Hodgkin-Huxley model, which first proposed the simplification of a neuron to a circuit. The conductances and steady-state voltages of ion channels, ion leaks across the cell membrane, membrane capacitance, and current in the form of ions going into the cell are used to predict membrane potential.

The Problem

Although I presented a possible problem as being the need to simulate behavior of a neuron under certain predetermined conditions, I realized that what I'm doing right this second is solving a problem in this industry. I feel that the fact that I had to be well-versed in python to truly grasp the nature of a neuroscience phenomenon is a problem, and I think these tools should be more widely accessible. That's why, by making a simulation that runs on this python code and simplifies things a bit, I feel that I've solved a problem of inaccessibility. Take whichever problem you will, but I think making these tools more accessible is more valuable than what I initially presented.
